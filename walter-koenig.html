Walter Koenig- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Education

Walter Koenig- Professional Career

In 1962, Walter Koenig made his cinematic debut in The Norman Vincent Peale Story. In 1974, he appeared in the film Nightmare Honeymoon. He was first featured as Pavel Chekov in Star Trek: The Motion Picture in 1979. Following this, he went on to appear in a number of Star Trek films. In Moontrap, he played Col. Jason Grant in 1989. In 1994, he was also seen in Star Trek Generations.

In 2006, he appeared in the film Mad Cowgirl. The next year, he appeared in InAlienable. This was followed by a role as Lassie in Blue Dream.

He appeared in the 2017 films Neil Stryker and the Tyrant of Time and Nobility. He played Milton Green in the film Diminuendo a year later. In 2019, he also appeared in the documentary Women in Motion. The actor was most recently seen in the 2020 film Unbelievable!!!!!.

In 1963, he made his debut television appearance in General Hospital. From 1963 through 1965, Koenig appeared in Mr. Novak. From 1967 through 1969, he played Pavel Chekov in Star Trek: The Original Series. In 1971, the actor appeared in the TV movie Goodbye, Raggedy Anne.

Following that, in 1974, he starred in The Questor Tapes. He played the part of Alfred Bester in Babylon 5 from 1994 to 1998. In 2018, the actor starred in the TV film Bone Eater.

In 2017, he even voiced Mr. Savic in Stretch Armstrong and the Flex Fighters. He went on to voice more Star Trek games as part of the Star Trek franchise.
