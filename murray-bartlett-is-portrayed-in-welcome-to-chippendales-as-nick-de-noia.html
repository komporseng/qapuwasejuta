Murray Bartlett Is Portrayed In Welcome To Chippendales As Nick De Noia

July 25, 2024
blog

In Hulu’s newest limited series, Welcome to Chippendales, renowned Australian actor Murray Bartlett portrays Nick De Noia.

Nick De Noia was an Emmy-winning American writer, director, and choreographer whose life was cut tragically short by a man hired by Somen “Steve” Banerjee, his former Chippendales Dance Club business partner.

On November 22, 2022, the first two episodes of Welcome to Chippendales will premiere on the popular streaming website Hulu.

Based on the best-selling book Deadly Dance: The Chippendales Murders by K. Scot Macdonald and Patrick MontesDeOca, this limited series was created. Robert Siegel, the creator of the program, is credited.

Here is the official synopsis of Welcome to Chippendales:

“WELCOME TO CHIPPENDALES is a sprawling true-crime drama that follows the incredible story of Somen “Steve” Banerjee, an Indian immigrant who became the unanticipated founder of the world’s largest male-stripping empire—and allowed nothing to stand in his way.”

Since catching glimpses of Murray Bartlett as Nick De Noia in the official video for Welcome to Chippendales, fans have been eager to see the actor’s interpretation of the intriguing character.

Who is Murray Bartlett exactly? Considering the actor who portrays Nick De Noia in the play Welcome to Chippendales.

Murray Bartlett, a renowned Australian actor 51 years old, began his career as Michael Freeman in the 1987 television series The Flying Doctors.

After portraying Julian Sinclair in the 2002 television series All My Children, he ascended to prominence. Bartlett is best known for his roles as Cyrus Foley in the 2007–2009 TV series Guiding Light, Dom Basaluzzo in the 2014–2015 TV series Looking, Michael “Mouse” Tolliver in the 2019 TV series Tales of the City, James Whitney in the 2013 film Girl Most Likely and Armond in the fan-favorite 2021 satirical TV series The White Lotus.

Dad and Dave: On Our Selection, The Three Stooges, Needle, The Stand-In, August, Muffled Love, Boys on Film 4: Protect Me from What I Want, Postmortem, Kingston Avenue, Stubborn, and Om are among the other notable features and short films in which he has appeared.

Home and Away, A Country Practice, Above the Law, The Secret Life of Us, Flight of the Conchords, The Good Wife, Madam Secretary, Iron Fist, RuPaul’s Drag Race Down Under, Murder Call, Flat Chat, All Saints, White Collar, The Ferals, and other shows have featured Murray Bartlett.

Additionally, he has appeared in a number of television films, including The Beast, The Tower, and Looking: The Movie. The actor will also appear in two upcoming series: The Last of Us and Extrapolations.
